About Me

Hello! I am a second-year Purdue AAE student who's also getting a minor in math. I have an interest in structures and materials, which is my planned specialization.

Outside of class, I am a lab supervisor for the Purdue 3D Printing Club, where I work with Purdue students of all majors to make their 3D design a reality. We have both FDM and SLA printers and often work with Purdue classes to make sure everyone can see their ideas come to life.

I am also a member of the Purdue Space Program, in the NASA Student Launch Organization, where we are building a rocket to compete in a NASA competition! I am an avionics and recovery team member, but I am also helping develop the IFVR, or In-Flight Video Recording, System which will record three different angles of the vehicle's flight.

One of my main goals is to help foster the next generation of STEM students! I am a Teaching Assistant for ENGR16100 and ENGR16200 at Purdue, the Honors First Year Engineering Class. I also am a Peer Mentor for this program, offering more personalized advice and help to engineering students.

STEM outreach is another important part of my life. I am a volunteer for Coder Dojo, a club which teaches the CAD program OnShape to members of the Lafayette Boy's and Girl's club. During non-COVID times, I also would teach Micro:Bit to local elementary and middle school students through the Algebra by 7th Grade (AB7G) program.

When I'm not participating in clubs or studying for classes, I work on personal projects. These range from building rockets and planes, to web development (e.g. this very website), to 3D printing and making things with my CNC router. Most of all, I'm just trying to learn as much as I can!.



Featured Projects




Testing Homemade Rocket Motors

This project consisted of making my own rocket motors, designing and building a stand capable of measuring their thrust, and finally calculating the specific impulse of each motor in order to enhance future rocket endeavors.


The CAD model of the rocket stand and the final product. A handle was added for enhanced portability.




A burned motor and the results of a full day of rocket science




Two different motor tests after change in nozzle geometry, resulting in specific impulse increase from 105s to 130s. Additionally, burntime was decreased resulting in more efficient motor



ESP8266 Based Messaging Service

The goal of this project was to create a messaging service using this website, an ESP8266 microcontroller, and an EInk display to display messages sent from the web. Additionally, this project included 3D modeling and printing a case, and creating an encrpytion algorithm written with PHP and a complementary decryption algorithm written with C++ for the ESP8266.


CAD Model and final product complete with status LED and button to dismiss new message




C++ program and assembling the final product




An example of the encrpyted messages. The PHP script also keeps track of who sent the message and when the message was sent. The same C++ program for the ESP8266 can decode this log of messages.
