Spring 2021

(14 cr)

AAE -

Undergraduate Research (Manufacturing and Testing Multifunctional Sensors)

One of the best aspects of Purdue is how many opportunities there are just a few questions away. I was extremely fortunate to gain hands on experience through this research assistance and made many valuable connections with graduate students. I was even able to play an integral role by designing a small machine to measure material to length, which was previously a tedious process.

AE30100 -

Signal Analysis For Aerospace Engineering

This class was plain cool. In much the same way that I thought that thermodynamics was cool, since it really made me feel like an engineer. There is something about signals and controls that made even some of the most mundane aspects of this class feel interesting. I enjoyed the final project, which allowed us to design a state space controller for a thrust vectoring rocket.

AAE33300 -

Fluid Mechanics

People oftne say fluid mechanics is hard and I have to say they aren't wrong. While this class was more difficult than most of the classes I have taken before, the difficulty was worth it as you really felt like you were solving real problems. At the same time, this class showed just how complicated real life is and how many approximations we must make due with.

AAE30000 -

Junior Seminar

This class was an 8 week seminar that took place during the latter half of the semester with focuses on some of the important soft skills engineers should have. For example, one of the topics was on diversity and inclusion, and another was on ethics.

AAE33301 -

Fluid Mechanics Lab

I love the labs at Purdue. Not only is there the inherent cool feeling you get using decade old machines and fixtures, but you gain a hands-on perspective of the material you typically only learn about in textbooks. This lab had an emphasis on aerodynamics, while some of the investigations used water as the fluid. We measured the head loss of pipe systems and the coefficient of drag of about anything we could stick in the wind tunnel.

AAE35200 -

Structural Analysis I

Much like fluid mechanics, this course showed just how much we need to approximate when it comes to analyzing complex systems. However, I found the derivations and theory behind the topics we learned fascinating, especially considering how much of it is used on structures we interact with everyday. This course also introducted fracture mechanics with concepts akin to those presented by Griffith in famous investigation on the properties of glass.

MA30300 -

Differential Equations And Partial Differential Equations For Engineering And The Sciences

This class was a review and reinforcement of some of the most important concepts learned in a previous differential equations course, with some new ideas thrown in such as Sturm-Liouville problems.

ENGR29600 -

Honors Peer Mentoring

Peer Mentoring remains one of my favorite parts of the honors engineering program. Both parties gain so much by sharing their experiences, and the students no doubt gain confidence by seeing other people make it through the program.

Fall 2020

(17 cr)

AAE20400 -

Aeromechanics II

Aeromechanics II is the mechanics of materials, sometimes called solid body mechanics. I enjoyed this class, with some of my favorite parts being beam analysis, graphing equations of displacement, and solving interesting statics problems.

AAE20401 -

Aeromechanics II Lab

This was my first lab at Purdue! I had fun breaking tensile test specimens, bending beams, and loading trusses. This course was a fun introduction to hands-on engineering and analysis of results.

AAE25100 -

Introduction to Aerospace Design

This course was a sophomore design project class which put fellow engineering students in groups to design either a space or air system. I chose the aircraft, since I felt that atmospheric flight has more room for creative spin and variance. I had a ton of fun and a really great group!

EAPS10600 -

Geosciences Cinema

This course was chosen because the professor was historically known to be one of the best at Purdue and the class was known to be tons of fun and learning. Both of these claims were true, this is one of my favorite courses I have taken at Purdue and I now know why hurricanes don't form at the equator.

ENGR29600 -

Honors Peer Mentoring

This course allowed experienced engineering students to give back to the program by mentoring new students. I was given four students to support through their first semester at Purdue and more importantly, their first semester as engineering students. It has always been a goal for me to give back to the program which has given me so much and this was a great chance to do so.

MA26600 -

Ordinary Differential Equations

Differential equations are some of the coolest aspects of math. In this course, we mainly covered first and second order equations, eventually moving into homogeneous systems of equations towards the end. I also really enjoyed the computer projects which combined what you learned and your programming skills to illustrate just how computationally complex DEs can be.

MA34100 -

Foundations of Analysis

The first thing my professor said to the class on our first day was "if you are an engineer, you are going to hate this class." I actually chose to take this class though, as it is not part of the aero curriculum and I enjoy math. I learned a great deal about how to be rigorous in math and I will always think of things from a real analysis perspective from now on.

AAE20300 -

Aeromechanics I

This was my first AAE course at Purdue! Since it was a Summer course it was taught at an accelerated rate. This class taught many of the fundamental physics concepts which are invaluable to an aerospace engineer. We also learned the "Basic Kinematic Equation," a Purdue coined term for a simplication of Euler equations for motion. Additionally, this course introduced statics and solving for reaction forces, a skill that would be essential for AAE20400.

Spring 2020

(16 cr)

ME20000 -

Thermodynamics I

This course was one of my favorite at Purdue! Known for being notoriously hard, I took this course two semesters early as I had already finished a great number of first year engineering required classes. This course was one of the first times I felt like I was actually learning engineering. I learned both the First and Second Law of Thermodynamics, as well as powerful concepts that can be used to analyze real life systems. Most of all, I learned how to look at a complex problem fundamentally and think carefully about my steps to finding a solution.

MA35100 -

Elementary Linear Algebra

This course was a wonderful introduction into what I call "creative math." As opposed to calculus, where many of the problems followed a formulaic procedure, this course had me thinking carefully about every problem. While many of the basic linear algebra topics and concepts were covered, this course had a focus on proving a variety of theorems and more abstract linear algebra topics. I still think back to this course and appreciate how it has changed my perspective of more abstract topics in math. And especially how it convinved me linear algebra is one of the most beautiful and elegant subjects there is.

CGT16300 -

Graphical Communication and Spatial Analysis

This course was an introductory course in computer aided design and how to communicate ideas visually. The primary program used in this course was Catia, which was definitely a change from the autodesk ecosystem I had grown used to. However, I learned a great deal about good practices when it came to desigining and organizing parts, and I enjoyed the technical drawing aspect of the class.

ENGR16200 -

Honors Inroduction to Physics and Engineering II

This course was Purdue First Year Engineering part two! While it followed the same basic outline of its first semester counterpart, there were more topics covered and put to use. This included some control theory and algorithms, angular momentum and velocity, as well as developing extensive Matlab models. I really appreciate the student these first year engineering classes have made me and I will never forget where I started.

HONR19902 -

The Evolution of Ideas II

This course was the second half of the 8 week seminar I took in Fall 2019 called HONR19901. This time around, the focus was more on independent learning and research, as well as effectively managing a small group in order to complete various projects over the 8 weeks.

HONR39900 -

History of Aerospace Technology

This course was a history of aerospace, from the first hot air balloons, to the future technologies that may one day make humans an interplanetary species. I loved this course because no matter how much I thought I knew a given airplane, I always learned something new. During this course we also built balsa wood gliders, explaining practically design choices like dihedral, aspect ratio, tail planes vs. canards.

Fall 2019

(20 cr)

HONR29900 -

Architecture of Chicago

This course was one of my favorite first semester courses! During the first weekend in October, a handful of students took of a trip to Chicago to explore both the modern and historic architecture. During this trip, the architecture of Mies Van Der Rhoe was explored, as well as the social implications of his work.

CS15900 -

C Programming

This course covered the C programming language, with various aspects such as pointers, user defined functions and dynamic memory allocation. Having already knew C before this course, it was a nice refresher, as well as learning some more features like conversion specifier widths and more.

ENGR10301 -

Engineering and Climate Change

This course was a 1 credit hour discussion based class that first centered around climate change related misconceptions and then on what individual efforts can be taken to reduce the impact humans have on the climate. This course was great, as I got to meet a number of different people from different majors all across Purdue.

HONR19901 -

The Evolution of Ideas

This course was an 8 week seminar dedicated to thinking about how we think. This epistemology based course combined group work, an older student mentor, and self guided learning to create a dynamic envvironment where all ideas were heard.

ENGR16100 -

Honors Introduction to Physics and Engineering I

This course was Purdue First Year Engineering part one! This is the course all first year students take before they transition to their respective major. This course coverd a variety of topics, but the most important lessons learned were soft skills. This included working as a group, having persistence when the class became difficult, and learning how to have a holistic approach to problem solving.

HONR19903 -

Interdisciplinary Approaches to Writing

This course was an elective writing class offered through the Purdue Honors College. The main theme that the writing and discussion revolved around was the idea of the anthropocene, an era in which humans and their action are the primary forces acting on the planet. This class incorporated all of the best aspects of ENGR10301 and HONR19901 resulting in one of my favorite classes taken in Fall 2019.

MA26100 -

Multivariate Calculus

This course was as its name suggests, calclulus III. I absolutely loved this class. In highschool, I would often search math on wikipedia and math stack exchange, and then come to my calclulus II teacher and ask if he could teach it to me. The syllabus of MA26100 was rich with these kind of topics, with a variety of ones I did not know of as well. Overall, this class was great and I just love how geometrically intuitive calc III is.
