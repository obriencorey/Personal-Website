What is this site for?

A number of things! I feel I have numerous skills and passions which are not articulated most effecitvely on a resume, so I thought I would try my best to showcase them here. Also, it's just fun to make a website. I have learned a ton about making a website responsive, nice to look at, and overall good design practices. Why Aerospace Engineering?

Great question! The short answer is this: Anyone who knows me would say this is a rhetorical question. I live, breathe, and speak aerospace. It's hard to put into words just how much I love it, I would just say wanting to be an aerospace engineer has always been one of my goals.

The long answer however, is for two very important reasons.

The first, and arguably more important, is that I want to inspire future generations of innovators much like past engineers have motivated me. For example, I'm sure when Lockheed was making the SR-71, they did not have my feelings in mind. But that piece of technology has made me want to push the envelope even further! I want to continue adding to the collective wealth of knowledge because one day we will need it. Earth has a time limit and the sooner we begin to become multiplanetary, the better. I doubt I will be the one to work on this technology, but maybe I will work on something that will inspire someone who will work on something that will inspire someone who will... ...work on this technology. That is my hope at least.

The second reason is because I like solving problems. I like being challenged with something few people have been challenged with. I regularly do things my own way just to have a taste of this feeling. I like a hard problem, and aerospace is full of them. School has been just a tiny taste of what is in store and I am excited for the future!


Favorite Plane?

All of them! I can't pick a favorite, but some of the best in my opinion are the English Electric Lightning, the Dassault Falcon 8x/9x, the Boeing 777-300ER (specifically the Air Canada light blue livery), anything made by Burt Rutan or Scaled Composites (I don't make the rules, they are just super cool), the Boeing 757-300, the Lockheed Model 12, the North American XB-70, the Martin Marietta X-24A, the Diamond DA-42, and many more. In other words, all planes.


Favorite Rocket?

Another hard one! Although, there is much less variety here. I would have to go with the Little Joe rockets used in the Apollo program. It just looks like something a little kid would design but served an essential purpose in the Apollo program.

Career Goals?

I find this is a related question to the "Why Aerospace?" one, but I do have some distinct goals. I would like to specialize in structures and materials and do some research within that discipline during my undergraduate studies. I would also like to get a Master's Degree in AAE, with a potential focus on structures and materials. I would then like to work for a company who is pushing the limits of what is possible. Whether that means working on revolutionary spacecraft, building the next generation of efficient air travel, or anything in between, I would love it. I'm just looking for some problems to solve that would make a difference.
